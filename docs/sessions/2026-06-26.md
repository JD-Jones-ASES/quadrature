## Autonomous session — the integral instrument off the time axis (ADR-0014)

Directive: build the *most complex* part first so the granular fill becomes simple. The deepest assumption in
the engine was that **time is the integration variable** and the instrument is the x–t/v–t/a–t stack —
everything a comprehensive course still needs (work `∫F dx`, thermo `∫P dV`, E&M `∫E·dl` / `∫dq/r²`, moment of
inertia) integrates over something else. Generalizing the "area = integral" thesis off the time axis is the
highest-leverage move, so that was the build.

Shipped (all gates green; 29 producer tests; parity 3355 samples; 211 KaTeX strings):

- **ADR-0014 — the area/integral instrument.** A new graph `kind:"area"`: a curve `f(u)` over a general axis
  `u`, its shaded area = the accumulated integral `g(u)=∫f du`, slope of `g` = `f`. New `AreaPlot` on the
  `Scenario` (temporal `x/v/a` made optional); `area_series {u,f,g,u_max}` schema shape gated by kind; a third
  proof kind **`integral`** (FTC slope, area=integral, memorized-result-falls-out, constant-integrand→
  rectangle); `render_area` + an `AreaPlot.svelte` island with a draggable cursor; axis-agnostic parity +
  solution gates (axis `t` or `u`). The browser axis variable is normalized to a canonical `u`.
- **Work–energy lesson** (`∫F dx`, regime 2, mechanics): area under F–x = work = ½mv²; SymPy proves ½mv² falls
  out of the integral; constant force collapses to the rectangle W=Fd. W=KE=9 J, v=3 m/s at the worked point.
- **Isothermal PV-work lesson** (`∫P dV`, regime 3, thermo — opens Phase 3): the *same* instrument on the
  **volume** axis, built with **no engine change** (model + spec + test only) — the demonstration that the hard
  instrument makes a domain's lessons simple. W=2740 J < the P₁ΔV rectangle (4989 J); two thermo reference
  formulas added (ideal-gas law, isothermal work); concept graph 26 → 28 nodes.

## Autonomous session (cont.) — mechanics breadth on the proven instruments

Owner picked "finish mechanics breadth." The point of the session's hard architecture is that breadth is now
authoring, not engineering — demonstrated by two lessons built with **no engine change** (model + spec + test):

- **Impulse–momentum** (regime 2): the **area instrument on the time axis** — area under the force–time pulse
  $F(t)=F_{\max}\sin(\pi t/\tau)$ is the impulse $\int F\,dt$, slope is the force, equals $\Delta p = m\Delta v$.
  The time-axis sibling of work–energy (∫F dx). Reuses `AreaPlot` verbatim. J = 2.55 N·s, Δv = 5.09 m/s.
- **Rotational kinematics** (regime 1): the **temporal stack with θ/ω/α labels** — the rotational equations are
  the straight-line ones relabeled, and $\omega^2=\omega_0^2+2\alpha\Delta\theta$ falls out of the integrals.
  Reuses `GraphStack`. Flywheel from rest, α=3 → ω=12 rad/s, θ=24 rad (3.82 rev).
