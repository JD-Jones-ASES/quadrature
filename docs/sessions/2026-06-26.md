## Autonomous session (cont.) — three area-instrument lessons: open fluids, deepen E&M, complete rotation

- **Electric potential energy** (`coulomb_pe.py`, regime 2, E&M): the **separation**-axis twin of
  `gravity_pe.py`. The energy to pull two opposite charges apart is the area under the Coulomb force
  $kq_1q_2/r^2$; because it is the *same* inverse square as gravity, the area to infinity **converges**, so the

- **Hydrostatic force on a wall** (`hydrostatic_force.py`, regime 2 — **opens the fluids domain's first

  triangle $\tfrac12\rho g w H^2$; SymPy shows the algebra's "average pressure × area" ($\bar P A$, centroid

  misconception is the **hydrostatic paradox** — force depends on depth & width, not on the volume of water
  behind the wall. $F=400$ kN ($5$ m wide, $4$ m deep). New `problems/fluids/` topic dir; $g=10$ as a magnitude

  $\tfrac12 I\omega^2$ falls out of the integral; constant-torque echo is $\tau_0\Delta\theta$. Completes the
  **rotation triad** (kinematics · moment of inertia · energy). $W=9$ J, $\omega=6$ rad/s for a $0.5$ kg·m²
  flywheel (same clean $9$ J as work–energy). Angular units: `rad` is dimensionless, so `τ = c·θ` (c in `N*m`)
