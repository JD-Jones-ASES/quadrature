## Shipped — collisions / momentum (ADR-0018)

- **A fifth graph instrument, `kind:"collision"`** — the first **two-state (before/after)** viz, distinct from
  the energy bars' single continuous cursor. A 1D two-body collision with the **coefficient of restitution `e`
  as the cursor** (1 = perfectly elastic … 0 = perfectly inelastic) and the incident mass `m1` a slider. Two
  grouped stacked-bar charts: **momentum** (before vs after — the total is pinned at every `e`, only the
  per-body split shifts) and **kinetic energy** (before vs after — equal only at `e=1`, collapsing as `e→0`
  with the gap shaded "lost"). Plus a before/after block schematic with velocity arrows.
- **The model** (`collision.py`, regime 2). Solves momentum conservation + the restitution relation for the
  closed-form finals
  `v1' = ((m1−e·m2)v1 + (1+e)m2·v2)/(m1+m2)`, `v2' = ((m2−e·m1)v2 + (1+e)m1·v1)/(m1+m2)`.
  The dual-register point: **momentum conservation is the time-integral of Newton's third law** — the contact
  forces are equal and opposite at every instant, so the impulses `J=∫F dt` cancel for *any* force profile,
  elastic or not (ties directly to the impulse lesson's `∫F dt`). Kinetic energy is different: the deformation
  work need not come back, leaving `½μ(1−e²)(Δv)²` behind (`μ=m1m2/(m1+m2)` the reduced mass). Proof
  `governing`, five checks: momentum conserved at every `e`; the restitution relation; the KE-loss identity;
  `e=1 ⇒` KE conserved; `e=0 ⇒` the common velocity `v_cm`.
- **Wired like the area/energy instruments — no new gate.** New `CollisionPlot` (base.py) + `collision`
  field on `Scenario`; emit `closed_form_collision`/`sample_collision_series` (axis `u`=e, m2/v1/v2 normalized
  out); `render_collision` static poster (momentum before/after; KE before/after-elastic/after-inelastic);
  build.py `kind=="collision"` branch + dimensional check; schema `collision_series {u,v1f,v2f,u_max}` + a
  `consts` object + an `if kind==collision` required-fields branch. The **kind-agnostic parity oracle** re-checks
  `v1f,v2f` (auto-detects axis `u`) — the only verification surface needed. `Collision.svelte` island, routed in
  `SolutionPlayer`.
- **One reference formula** — `mom-inelastic-v` (the perfectly-inelastic common velocity
  `(m1v1+m2v2)/(m1+m2)`), edged to `mom-momentum` (derived-from) and `mom-impulse` (related-to). NB: the
  coefficient of restitution itself (`(v2'−v1')/(v1−v2)`) can't be a reference formula — it's a ratio of
  velocity *differences*, and the units checker collapses `m/s − m/s → 0`, giving `0/0`. The common-velocity
  form (pure sums) is dimensionally clean.
