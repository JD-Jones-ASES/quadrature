## Shipped — thin-lens ray-diagram instrument (ADR-0024)

- **A seventh graph instrument, `kind:"lens"`** — the first *geometric construction* (no time axis, no
  integration axis, no mode ladder). A converging lens of fixed focal length f at the origin; the **object
  distance d_o is the cursor**, an **object height h_o** slider. The island (`Lens.svelte`) draws the three
  principal rays (parallel→F′, chief through the centre, focal→parallel) with **Liang–Barsky viewport clipping**
  and reads off where they cross — the image — recomputing live. As d_o crosses f the image flips
  **real/inverted** (projector) ↔ **virtual/upright/enlarged** (magnifying glass), with dashed
  backward-extension rays in the virtual case. Quick-set chips (d_o = 2f, between f and 2f, inside f).
- **The model** (`thin_lens.py`, regime 3). Parity-verified closed forms d_i = d_o f/(d_o−f),
  h_i = −h_o f/(d_o−f), m = −f/(d_o−f), over the canonical `u` axis (= d_o), so the **kind-agnostic parity
  oracle re-checks them with no gate change**. **f is a fixed constant, never a slider** — the singularity at
  d_o=f never moves, and the sampler is chosen to straddle it and fails loud if a sample lands within 1e-7 of f.
  Proof `governing`, five checks: d_i satisfies 1/d_o+1/d_i=1/f; the chief ray gives m=−d_i/d_o; the parallel
  and focal rays give the *same* m (the three constructions agree at one point — which IS the thin-lens
  equation); m = f/(f−d_o). The second register is honestly **geometry**, not calculus (Fermat's stationary
  path noted as the calculus underneath, not derived).
- **Wired like the standing-wave instrument — no new gate.** New `LensPlot` (base.py) + `lens` on `Scenario`;
  `closed_form_lens`/`closed_form_params_lens`/`sample_lens_series` (axis `u`, singularity guard);
  `render_lens` static poster (the ray diagram converging to the real image); build.py `kind=="lens"` branch +
  dimensional check on d_i/h_i/m; schema `lens_series {u,di,hi,m,u_max}` + a `kind=="lens"` required-fields
  branch. `Lens.svelte` routed in `SolutionPlayer`.
- **A per-lesson register-label override.** Optional `calculus.register_label` (default "Calculus"): the
  thin-lens lesson relabels its second tab **"Ray diagram"** — threaded through `SolutionPlayer` (tab + aria)
  and `PracticeQuestion` (the practice step-through tab). One optional schema string; the general hook for any
